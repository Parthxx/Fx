Echoes of the Ultras

Legendary Fan Groups

Boixos Nois (FC Barcelona)

Founded in the early 1980s, Boixos Nois has become synonymous with Barcelona’s ultra culture. Their political activism, supporting Catalan independence, adds a layer of intensity to their matches.

Ultras Sur (Real Madrid)

A right-wing group known for their passionate, if controversial, support for Real Madrid. Ultras Sur has had a tumultuous history, often associated with violence and political extremism.

Chelsea Headhunters (Chelsea FC)

One of the most notorious hooligan groups in English football, Chelsea Headhunters made their name in the 1980s for violent clashes with rival firms, especially during the height of the hooliganism era.

Newell's Old Boys (Rosario, Argentina)

A fierce fanbase in Argentina, Newell's Old Boys are known for their intimidating presence and passionate support. Their rivalry with Rosario Central makes the Clásico Rosarino one of the most heated derbies in South America.

The Red Army (Manchester United)

A dominant force at Old Trafford, The Red Army represents Manchester United's ultra culture. Known for their unwavering support, they are key to the high-energy atmosphere of the stadium.

Northside Dortmund (Borussia Dortmund)

As a part of the famous Yellow Wall, Northside Dortmund is instrumental in creating one of the most electric atmospheres in European football. Their support for Borussia Dortmund is passionate and unrelenting.

Football Rivalries That Shaped History

Boixos Nois vs. Ultras Sur (El Clásico)

The rivalry between Boixos Nois and Ultras Sur symbolizes the larger Barcelona vs. Real Madrid clash. These two groups' bitter feud is marked by their opposing political views and their fierce battles for supremacy in Spanish football.

Chelsea Headhunters vs. West Ham United (London Derby)

The Chelsea Headhunters are infamous for their violent encounters with rival hooligan firms, particularly West Ham United's ICF. Their rivalry reached its peak in the 1980s.

Newell's Old Boys vs. Rosario Central (Clásico Rosarino)

The Clásico Rosarino between Newell's Old Boys and Rosario Central is one of the most passionate rivalries in Argentina, with both groups known for their extreme fan loyalty and frequent violent clashes.

The Red Army vs. Manchester City (Manchester Derby)

The rivalry between The Red Army and Manchester City’s fanbase has grown in intensity over the years, with the derby often turning into a battle for bragging rights in the city.

Northside Dortmund vs. Schalke 04 (Revierderby)

Northside Dortmund is at the heart of the Revierderby against Schalke 04, one of the fiercest rivalries in Germany. The match is a showcase of footballing passion, with both sets of fans creating a charged atmosphere.

Hooliganism and Football Gangs

Boixos Nois

Though mostly political, Boixos Nois have been involved in violent incidents during El Clásico and other key matches. Their aggressive actions stem from both football rivalries and political motives.

Ultras Sur

Known for their violent clashes with rival fan groups, especially Boixos Nois, Ultras Sur is infamous for their involvement in football hooliganism and far-right activism.

Chelsea Headhunters

Renowned for their hooligan activities in the 1980s, Chelsea Headhunters were notorious for street fights and their influence in the international hooligan scene. Their violence was often linked to Chelsea’s away games.

Newell's Old Boys

While Newell's Old Boys is largely known for its passionate support, their clashes with Rosario Central fans have led to frequent violence, making them a key figure in South American football hooliganism.

The Red Army

While largely focused on passionate support, The Red Army has also been involved in some confrontations with rival fans. They maintain a strong presence in both domestic and international matches.

Northside Dortmund

Northside Dortmund is part of the infamous Yellow Wall and has been involved in intense rivalries. They have had several run-ins with rival fanbases, particularly during high-stakes matches.
